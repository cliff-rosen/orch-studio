Plotpoint Competitive Landscape — Top 3 Direct Competitors

Status: draft. Sections marked TK are not yet written. Findings cited inline reference stores/findings/.

Summary

Plotpoint sits in a competitive landscape with three direct competitors who occupy meaningfully different positions: Linear (engineering-led, opinionated), Asana (enterprise generalist), and ClickUp (horizontal SMB consolidator). Each is strong on different axes; none is winning on all. Our differentiation thesis (TK — to be written in synthesis stage) should target the gap each leaves open.

Why these three

We surveyed five candidates and dropped two:

  • Notion — competes on documents and knowledge, with project planning as a secondary surface. Different center of gravity.
  • Trello — different segment. Personal/light kanban, not the structured planning we target.

Comparative findings

Product depth

  • Linear — best-in-class on speed and keyboard UX, but deliberately narrow.
  • Asana — broadest surface; generalist; depth varies by surface.
  • ClickUp — widest surface but G2 reviews flag complexity and performance.

Pricing model

  • Linear — simple flat tiers, no per-feature gating below enterprise.
  • Asana — heavily feature-gated SKUs; key features upsell.
  • ClickUp — Free Forever + value pricing; bundle-replacement pitch.

Target market

  • Linear — engineering-led product teams.
  • Asana — mid-market and enterprise, cross-functionally.
  • ClickUp — SMB and growing mid-market, horizontally.
