# Plotpoint Competitive Landscape — Top 3 Direct Competitors

> Status: **draft**. Sections marked TK are not yet written. Findings cited inline reference `stores/findings/`.

## Summary

Plotpoint sits in a competitive landscape with three direct competitors who occupy meaningfully different positions: Linear (engineering-led, opinionated), Asana (enterprise generalist), and ClickUp (horizontal SMB consolidator). Each is strong on different axes; none is winning on all. Our differentiation thesis (TK — to be written in synthesis stage) should target the gap each leaves open.

## Why these three

We surveyed five candidates and dropped two:

- **Notion** — competes on documents and knowledge, with project planning as a secondary surface. Different center of gravity. (See `stores/competitors/comp-004.json`)
- **Trello** — different segment. Personal/light kanban, not the structured planning we target. Also broadly subsumed by ClickUp's free tier in the SMB space. (See `stores/competitors/comp-005.json`)

The three we kept all directly compete for buyers who want structured project planning beyond simple kanban or generic tasks.

## Profiles

Each profile is in `stores/competitors/<id>.profile.md` (Document column on the competitors store). One-line summaries here:

- **Linear** — fast, opinionated issue tracker for software teams. (`comp-001.profile.md`)
- **Asana** — mature, enterprise-friendly work management generalist. (`comp-002.profile.md`)
- **ClickUp** — aggressively horizontal, "one app to replace them all" SMB platform. (`comp-003.profile.md`)

## Comparative findings

### Product depth

- **Linear** is best-in-class on speed and keyboard UX, but deliberately narrow. Engineering-only by design. (`find-001`)
- **Asana** is the broadest — projects, portfolios, OKRs, workflows, automations. Generalist; depth varies by surface. (`find-004`)
- **ClickUp** has the widest surface, but G2 reviews persistently flag complexity and performance issues. (`find-007`)

### Pricing model

- **Linear** is simple: free up to 10 users, $8 starter, $14 business. No per-feature gating below enterprise — distinctive among peers. (`find-002`)
- **Asana** uses heavily feature-gated tiers ($13.49 → $30.49 → custom). Workflows, custom fields, and goals are upsells. (`find-005`)
- **ClickUp** undercuts with Free Forever and $7-$12 paid tiers. Pitch is bundle replacement: "replace N tools with one." (`find-008`)

### Target market

- **Linear** targets engineering-led product teams. Cross-functional teams (marketing, ops) are out of scope. (`find-003`)
- **Asana** sells to mid-market and enterprise, cross-functionally. ~85% of revenue from teams of 100+. (`find-006`)
- **ClickUp** targets SMB and growing mid-market, horizontally — price-sensitive buyers consolidating tools. (`find-009`)
