# Evolution — Memo to Conv-1

Historical record of how framings in the original teaser memo (`sources/memo-original-teaser.md`) were refined through `sources/conv-1.md`. Moved here from `case-orchestrator-studios.md` during the Phase 2.5+ cleanup — the refinements are baked into the current canon, so the historical table is preserved as provenance rather than active material.

| Original framing | Refined framing |
|---|---|
| "Bottleneck is cognition, not orchestration" | Cognitive capacity was always the binding constraint; what's new is that the *cognitive-for-dollars exchange rate collapsed* for a wide range of work |
| "Most teams will experience fleet mgmt and never notice cog lev" | The confusion isn't whether cog lev exists — it's that teams mistake fleet mgmt for the top of the stack |
| "The delegation decision *is* the cognitive-leverage decision" | Delegation frees capacity; cognitive leverage is the decision of *where that capacity gets redirected* |
| "Plan: a delegation roadmap" | Plan: delegation map + a conscious stance on depth vs. breadth reinvestment. N is an output of both, not an input. |
| Implicit S-curve shape | Three-region Ricardian sort (agent-better / wash / operator-better) that derives the shape. Later collapsed to one threshold `t_i` in the Phase 2.5+ clean canon. |
| "Quality drops slower than N grows, so total climbs" | True, but reframed: reinvestment is reallocation, not addition; every step trades quality somewhere for a larger gain elsewhere. |
| "Success-rate cliff" (where products stop functioning) | Parked as a modeling assumption — the curve has no derivable interior maximum; external caps do the bounding. |
| Fleet mgmt "reasons about how thin we can stretch ourselves" | Fleet mgmt optimizes the *how* within fixed reach; cog lev treats reach as the decision variable. |
| Novelty claim implicit throughout | Explicitly narrowed: the two drivers (A: cheap *and* capable; B: configurable labor). Delegation, comparative advantage, and quality/volume tradeoffs are all old. |
| Studio's current state: "running two products as fleet mgmt shop" | Refined: N=1→N=2 was itself a small, unspoken cognitive-leverage move (the exchange-rate shift applied to fixed inputs); studio has just ridden it opportunistically since. |
