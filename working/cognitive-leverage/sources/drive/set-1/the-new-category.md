**COGNITIVE LEVERAGE**

*The category above orchestration — and the opportunity to own it*

**The Shift**

2026 was the year of orchestration. AI made spinning up capable, autonomous agent systems fast and cheap. The transaction cost of starting a new delegation relationship collapsed to near zero. The economics of driving that relationship dropped to a fraction of what they were.

Those two forces together revealed something nobody had a word for yet.

**The New Bottleneck**

Portfolios will expand until they hit the only remaining constraint: the cognitive capacity of the human directing them.

Not access to agents. Not cost of execution. **You.** Your ability to direct, absorb, scan, and steer across an expanding set of workstreams simultaneously. This has always been a constraint — but it was never the *binding* constraint before. Now it is.

| WHAT IT IS Cognitive Leverage is the ratio of output generated to cognitive capital invested. In a world of infinite cheap agents, this becomes the primary competitive variable for every knowledge worker, operator, and entrepreneur. The person who can run ten high-output workstreams on the same cognitive budget someone else uses to run two doesn't win because they're smarter. They win because they're better at this specific skill. | WHAT THE MARKET NEEDS Nobody is answering these questions yet: What does high cognitive leverage look like — who has it, what do they do differently? How do I measure mine — is there a framework, a diagnostic? How do I improve it — methodology, practice, tooling? What does a high-leverage portfolio look like vs. a bloated one? |
