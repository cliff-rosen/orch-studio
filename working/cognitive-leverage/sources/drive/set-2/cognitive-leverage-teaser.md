The New Leverage Game

Here is a thought experiment.

Think back to any point in your career when you had good people working for you. Now imagine that on some ordinary Tuesday morning you could have cloned any of them — instantly, for free, as many times as you wanted.

Would you have done it?

Obviously yes. And whoever did it first would have had an almost unfair advantage over everyone who couldn't.

You would have kept cloning until the complexity of managing them all started eating the gains. At some point the returns would flatten and you'd find your ceiling. But you would have pushed hard to find that ceiling, and a whole ecosystem would have emerged to help you — better tools, better practices, better infrastructure — all organized around getting the most out of that new capability.

That moment is now. AI agents are the clones. They're not perfect and they're not free, but they're close enough that the math has fundamentally changed. The resource you delegate to no longer takes months to find and months to train. It doesn't carry a fixed cost whether or not it's being used. You can have one or a hundred, with whatever skill profile the work requires, spun up in minutes.

Over the next two years, people and organizations are going to start recognizing this and building toward it deliberately. And right alongside that, an ecosystem of tools, frameworks, and infrastructure will emerge to support them. 

I'm not being hyperbolic when I say this is a gold rush moment. The infrastructure doesn't exist yet. The best practices haven't been written. The picks and shovels haven't been built. We should talk about where we want to be standing when this becomes obvious to everyone.
