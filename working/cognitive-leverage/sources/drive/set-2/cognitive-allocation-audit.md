# **The Cognitive Allocation Audit**

## **What It Is**

A structured diagnostic of every active claim on your cognitive capital — agents, projects, relationships, decisions in progress — that produces actionable arbitrage signals rather than just a snapshot. The audit maps the full portfolio, including invisible holdings: things consuming background load that never appear on any list.

---

## **What It Measures**

For each item in the portfolio, the audit captures:

* **Baseline carrying cost** — what it takes to sustain this at minimum viable engagement, broken down by engagement type (ambiguity resolution, novel judgment, quality gate failure, horizon drift, affirmation)  
* **Return and horizon** — what this is actually generating, and when  
* **Leverage ratio** — how autonomous is the return stream; how much of it requires you  
* **Horizon debt** — how far has the work run past your current understanding  
* **Assimilation debt** — how much output has accumulated that hasn’t entered your model  
* **Activation threshold** — how much above baseline would unlock meaningfully higher return

---

## **The Five Arbitrage Signals**

**1\. Underlevered positions** High-return projects still requiring excessive cognitive injection because no investment has been made in moving them toward autonomy. The arbitrage is architectural: invest now to reduce carrying cost permanently.

**2\. Zombie positions** Projects consuming baseline cognitive cost whose returns don’t justify continuation — persisting only because culling requires an active decision. The arbitrage is simply stopping.

**3\. Starved positions** Projects sitting just below their activation threshold. You’re paying the carrying cost but not reaching the productive zone. A modest, focused injection would unlock disproportionate return. The arbitrage is temporary concentration.

**4\. Mistyped engagement** Affirmation masquerading as judgment — real cognitive capital spent on what is actually a trust calibration problem. The arbitrage is recognition and re-engineering.

**5\. Hidden load** Background carrying cost on items not formally in the portfolio. Once surfaced, they compete on the same scorecard as everything else — and often lose, which is the point.

---

## **The Core Question**

Where is the market for my attention mispriced?

The audit answers this with specificity. Not “I’m too busy” — but which positions are misallocated, which debts are compounding, and where a reallocation would generate the highest return on the next unit of cognitive capital deployed.

---

## **Why This Is Different From Productivity Review**

A productivity review asks: *how much got done?*

The cognitive allocation audit asks: *was the attention that made it happen the best use of that attention — given everything else competing for it, including things that aren’t agents at all?*

The portfolio always has an opportunity cost dimension. The audit makes it visible.
